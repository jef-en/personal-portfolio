    01. Introduction.

    Go Jeep is a jeepney booking app developed in response to the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic. Its primary objective is to provide a safer, more secure transportation experience for passengers and jeepney drivers by offering essential features such as contact tracing and advance booking. The app allows users to book a jeepney ride in advance, reducing the need for crowded jeepney terminals and minimizing physical interaction between passengers. In addition to offering a safer booking experience, Go Jeep integrates a contact tracing feature that helps track passenger movements for health and safety purposes. This ensures that in the event of an exposure, authorities can quickly trace individuals who may have been in close contact with an infected passenger, improving public health response efforts.

    02. Problem Statement.

    Identifying the Problem.

    Operating jeepney transportation in a post-COVID world—often referred to as the "new normal"—presents significant challenges. Jeepneys, which are a primary mode of public transport in the Philippines, are inherently risky due to their nature as shared, crowded spaces. In a world still affected by the pandemic, passengers cannot easily determine if they are traveling with someone who may be infected. The lack of consistent and effective health screening at entry points, coupled with limited social distancing options inside jeepneys, increases the potential for virus transmission.


    However, completely halting jeepney operations is not a feasible solution, as jeepney drivers rely on daily earnings to support their families. This creates a difficult balance between maintaining public health and supporting the livelihoods of drivers and other workers in the transportation sector. Thus, the challenge lies in finding a way to keep jeepney services running safely, without putting passengers and drivers at excessive risk, while also ensuring that drivers can continue earning a living. The solution will need to address both health and economic concerns in a sustainable way.

    03. Goals.

    The primary goal of Go Jeep is to provide a safer, more efficient jeepney transportation experience in the wake of the COVID-19 pandemic. The app aims to address the challenges of public transportation during the "new normal" by offering features such as advance booking and contact tracing, which promote safety, reduce crowding, and support health protocols. By allowing users to book jeepney rides in advance, Go Jeep minimizes physical contact between passengers and drivers, ensuring a safer travel environment. The contact tracing feature helps track and trace passengers in case of potential exposure, supporting public health efforts in managing the spread of the virus. Additionally, Go Jeep seeks to ensure that jeepney drivers can continue to earn a livelihood while adhering to health and safety standards. The platform’s goal is to enhance the overall public transportation system by providing a user-friendly, accessible, and safer alternative to traditional jeepney travel, thus improving both public safety and convenience.

    05. Learning and Takeaway.

    My primary takeaway from this project is that when creating products, they should not only fulfill a market need but also aim to solve real-world problems that have an impact on society. Through the development of Go Jeep, I’ve learned the importance of designing solutions that address both individual and collective challenges. In this case, the problem of safely navigating public transportation during the COVID-19 pandemic required a thoughtful approach to both user safety and the livelihood of jeepney drivers. This project taught me the value of user-centric design, where the needs of the community—such as health security, convenience, and accessibility—are at the forefront of the development process. By integrating features like advance booking and contact tracing, Go Jeep doesn’t just provide a service, but actively contributes to public health and safety.


    Another key takeaway is the importance of sustainability and adaptability in design. The solution should not only address immediate concerns (such as pandemic-related safety) but also be adaptable to future challenges, ensuring long-term relevance. This experience has reinforced the idea that, as a technopreneur, my responsibility is not just to innovate but to create products that have a meaningful and positive impact on the community.
