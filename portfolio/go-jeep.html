    01. Introduction.

    Project Overview

    Go Jeep is a jeepney booking app addressing COVID-19 challenges by enabling advance bookings and integrating contact tracing for safer, more secure transportation. It minimizes crowding, reduces physical interactions, and supports public health efforts by tracking passenger movements.

    Responsibilities.

    • UI/UX Designer

    Project Duration.

    July 2022

    Tools.

    • Figma

    02. Problem Statement.

    Identifying the Problem.

    Operating jeepneys in the post-COVID "new normal" is challenging due to crowded spaces and limited health measures, increasing virus risks. Halting operations isn’t viable, as drivers depend on daily earnings. The solution must balance public health and drivers' livelihoods sustainably.

    03. Goals.

    Primary Goals

    Go Jeep enhances jeepney transportation in the post-COVID era with features like advance booking and contact tracing to ensure safety, reduce crowding, and support health protocols. It promotes safer travel while enabling drivers to maintain their livelihoods, offering a user-friendly and efficient alternative to traditional jeepney services.

    05. Learning and Takeaway.

    What Did I Learn?

    My primary takeaway from this project is that when creating products, they should not only fulfill a market need but also aim to solve real-world problems that have an impact on society. Through the development of Go Jeep, I’ve learned the importance of designing solutions that address both individual and collective challenges. In this case, the problem of safely navigating public transportation during the COVID-19 pandemic required a thoughtful approach to both user safety and the livelihood of jeepney drivers. This project taught me the value of user-centric design, where the needs of the community—such as health security, convenience, and accessibility—are at the forefront of the development process. By integrating features like advance booking and contact tracing, Go Jeep doesn’t just provide a service, but actively contributes to public health and safety.


    Another key takeaway is the importance of sustainability and adaptability in design. The solution should not only address immediate concerns (such as pandemic-related safety) but also be adaptable to future challenges, ensuring long-term relevance. This experience has reinforced the idea that, as a technopreneur, my responsibility is not just to innovate but to create products that have a meaningful and positive impact on the community.
