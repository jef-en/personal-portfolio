    01. Introduction.

    Project Overview.

    Shift Travel is a user-centric UI/UX project designed to provide travelers with seamless, step-by-step guidance throughout their journey. By offering clear, intuitive, and actionable instructions, Shift Travel helps users navigate their travel experience with ease, ensuring a smooth and efficient passage from start to finish. Whether for business or leisure, the platform simplifies complex travel logistics by consolidating all necessary information—such as routes, transportation options, and real-time updates—into one easy-to-follow interface.

    02. Problem Statement.

    Identifying the Problem.

    Traveling can be a stressful and uncertain experience, especially for solo travelers, backpackers, or those unfamiliar with their destination. Many travelers struggle to find clear, reliable, and detailed instructions on how to reach their destination, often relying on vague online guides or generic navigation tools. While platforms like Google Maps provide basic route information, they fail to account for essential details such as local fees, entrance charges, transportation options, and alternative routes.


    For backpackers and solo travelers—who may not have access to private transportation or prefer to travel using local options—this lack of comprehensive guidance can lead to confusion, delays, and unnecessary stress. Furthermore, relying on general travel instructions or directions from various sources can result in fragmented information, making it difficult to plan a smooth, efficient journey.


    What’s missing is a solution that not only provides clear, step-by-step navigation but also integrates all the critical travel details—like costs, transportation modes, and real-time updates—into one cohesive, easy-to-follow platform.

    03. Goals

    The primary goal of this project is to create an intuitive and user-friendly UI design tailored for solo travelers and backpackers, providing them with detailed, reliable instructions on how to reach popular tourist destinations in the Philippines. The platform will guide users step-by-step, offering clear and practical directions, including transportation options, estimated travel times, entry fees, and other important details that are often overlooked by traditional navigation tools.


    The secondary goal is to build a product that aligns with the principles of Technopreneurship—combining innovation with business potential. By developing a unique travel solution, the project aims to fill a gap in the market for personalized, easy-to-follow travel assistance, while also creating opportunities for entrepreneurship in the tech space.

    04. Design Process.

    05. Learning and Takeaway.

    One of the key takeaways from this project is the understanding that as a designer or developer, I am not creating for myself, but for others. My professor often emphasizes, "Once you enter the IT world, you're not designing or creating for yourself, but for your users." This is an essential mindset to adopt, especially when designing user interfaces. It's crucial to consider the needs, preferences, and challenges of the target audience to ensure the solution is truly effective and usable.


    For this project, I will be designing a tool for solo and backpack travelers—people with unique needs and experiences. Therefore, I must prioritize usability and accessibility in the design, ensuring that the interface is simple to navigate, visually appealing, and intuitive, even for those unfamiliar with technology.


    Additionally, another important learning is the necessity of designing for the CLI (Common Look and Feel) in the tech industry. This means ensuring that the design adheres to standard user interface principles, creating a consistent, familiar experience that users can quickly adapt to. It's not just about aesthetics but also about providing a seamless experience across devices and platforms, especially in the context of travel, where users might be using the app in unfamiliar environments with varying internet connections.
