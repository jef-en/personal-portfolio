    01. Introduction.

    What is Dr. Abels Adventures.

    "Dr. Abel's Adventures" is an engaging and educational game based learning application tailored specifically for Grade 8 students at Camarines Sur National High School. The game aims to enhance students' understanding and awareness of crucial concepts related to the prevention and control of common communicable diseases.


    Through immersive gameplay, students will learn about the transmission routes, symptoms, and preventive measures of common communicable diseases such as influenza, tuberculosis, malaria, and many more. The game incorporates visually appealing graphics, informative text, and engaging audio to provide an immersive learning experience. Dr. Abel's Adventures aims to make the learning process enjoyable and accessible, transforming complex concepts into engaging gameplay experiences. By combining entertainment with education, the game empowers students to become proactive in preventing the spread of communicable diseases and promotes a lifelong passion for scientific knowledge.

    What is Game Based Learning.

    Game-based learning refers to an engaging approach to education that employs games as a means to enhance student learning. By participating in these games, students actively acquire knowledge, thereby fostering the development of vital cognitive abilities such as critical thinking and problem solving. Game-based learning can encompass both digital and non-digital games as well as simulations, enabling students to directly experience the learning process.

    Purpose.

    The main purpose of this study is to help enhance the students’ health literacy, particularly on the prevention and control of communicable diseases to avoid, if not lessen, the chances of another global health crisis like the COVID-19 pandemic in the country. By the end of the study, it aims to determine if game-based learning was effective in teaching Health education, specifically about preventing and controlling communicable diseases, to Grade 8 students of Camarines Sur National High School (CSNHS).

    Objectives.

    The main objective of the study was to develop a game-based learning application to enhance the knowledge and awareness of the Grade 8 students about the concepts concerning the prevention and control of communicable diseases and to assess whether the implementation of a game-based learning environ ment was an effective tool in teaching the students who are taking MAPEH subject. To achieve the main objective, the following specific objectives were accomplished;

    • Incorporated the topics from the Health component of the MAPEH (Music, Arts, Physical Education and Health) subject of the Department of Education (DepEd) MELC: K to 12 Most Essential Learning Competencies about preven tion and control of communicable diseases in the developed game.
    • Evaluated the usability and the effectiveness of the game based application through usability testing.
    • Developed a 2D game-based application to serve as a learn ing tool.

    Scope and Limitations.

    This study focuses on the Health component of the MAPEH subject, specifically the prevention and control of communicable diseases, as outlined in the Department of Education’s K to 12 Most Essential Learning Competencies (MELC) for the 3rd Quarter of the 2022-2023 school year. It targets Grade 8 students at Camarines Sur National High School, excluding other Grade 8 students or schools outside Naga City. The study examines the demonstration of understanding in disease prevention and control for individual wellness, using a game-based learning application supported only on Android mobile platforms.

    02. Methodology.

    Development Model.

    This study used the Successive Approximation Model (SAM) for system development, an agile, iterative approach that allows for continuous assessment and improvement. SAM consists of three phases: (1) Preparation, where project data and context are gathered; (2) Iterative Design, where prototypes are created and evaluated; and (3) Iterative Development, where the prototype is refined until the desired outcome is achieved.

    Use Case Diagram.

    Upon opening the game, the players will be presented on the home screen with a chapter select button; If they Select a chapter, the chapter will load, and they will have the options to: (1) Restart the chapter, (2) Go back to Home Screen, or (3) Exit the game

    Game Flow Diagram.

    Upon opening the game, players are presented with a main menu to either close the game or select a chapter by entering its code. While playing, players interact with NPCs:

    • Patient NPCs: Players engage in a Q&A to answer health-related questions. Correct answers update progress and unlock further interactions, while incorrect answers result in losing hearts. If they run out of hearts, the game ends, and they can choose to return to the main menu or retry.
    • Information NPCs: Provide directions, instructions, and fun facts related to the current chapter.
    • Chests: Present a single Q&A. Correct answers allow players to regain lost hearts.

    03. Systems Design.

    Integration of Lecture.

    The game is based on the Physical Education and Health 8 book provided by Camarines Sur National High School (CSNHS) and aligns with the Department of Education’s K to 12 MELC for Grade 8 Health. It features a quiz-style environment where players answer questions on topics such as communicable diseases, types of germs and disease agents (bacteria, viruses, fungi, parasites), their transmission, prevention methods, causes, symptoms, effects, and common misconceptions about these diseases.

    Game Title Inspiration.

    The title Dr. Abel’s Adventures was inspired by Dr. Abelardo Aguilar, a Filipino physician from Iloilo who discovered the antibiotic properties of Streptomyces Erythreus in 1949. His work led to the development of Erythromycin, a widely used antibiotic, though he was not credited for the discovery. The proponents chose to honor Dr. Aguilar to highlight his significant, though overlooked, contribution to medicine and to encourage young learners to appreciate the important achievements of past generations.

    Game Storyline.

    After studying medicine for four years at Ateneo de Naga University, Abel decided to complete his residency at the local hospital. He spent three years training at the same medical center and became a licensed doctor. However, before he could even begin practicing, Abel received devastating news. His beloved grandfather had passed away due to lung complications caused by a virus and poor lifestyle choices. Heartbroken, Abel decided to put his career on hold and tend to his grandfather’s house, which had been left to him. When he arrived back in his hometown, he was greeted by the province’s mayor, who expressed his condolences and shared his concerns about the state of the town’s healthcare system. The mayor explained that many people in the area were struggling with health issues due to the lack of proper services and medical personnel. Curious about Abel’s background, the mayor asked about his past jobs and hobbies. Learning that Abel was a recent medical graduate, the mayor saw an opportunity to improve the town’s healthcare services. He offered Abel a plot of land to build his clinic and asked for help solving the town’s health problems. Although initially hesitant, Abel accepted the mayor’s offer and vowed to do his best to provide the town with the healthcare it deserved.

    Gameplay and Mechanics.

    Inspired by Pokemon and Stardew Valley, Dr. Abel’s Adventures follows Dr. Abel, a newly graduated doctor, as he builds a clinic in his hometown. To gather resources and services, he must first help various NPCs by diagnosing their health problems. The game features four chapters, each focusing on different pathogens (bacteria, viruses, fungi, and parasites), with scenarios covering characteristics, transmission, causes, myths, and prevention. Players interact with two types of NPCs: Patient NPCs, who need help identifying their health issues, and Information NPCs, who provide useful chapter-related details. Success in diagnosing Patient NPCs leads to resources for building the clinic, while incorrect answers cause the player to lose trust (represented by hearts). Losing all hearts results in game over. Chests scattered throughout the map offer trivia questions that can restore lost trust. To win, players must correctly diagnose all Patient NPCs and gather the necessary resources to complete the clinic.

    04. Testing Approach.

    Research Design.

    This study employed a Non-Equivalent Group Quasi-Experimental design, where two groups were selected to compare learning outcomes. The experimental group, 8-Mars, used the game-based learning tool to study communicable diseases, while the control group, 8-Mercury, followed the traditional teacher-led lesson plan. This design allowed the researchers to assess whether using the game-based tool significantly improved students' learning outcomes.

    Conduct of Study.

    The system was tested using pre-test and post-test questionnaires. For the experimental group (8-Mars), a pre-test on each chapter was administered before playing the game, followed by the game, and a post-test afterward to assess their learning. The control group (8-Mercury) followed the teacher’s lesson plan without using the game. On the final day, both groups took a summative test on the topics covered.


    The researchers then compared the pre-test and post-test scores to evaluate learning outcomes for both groups. Finally, the experimental group's results were compared to the control group to determine if the game-based learning tool significantly improved learning outcomes compared to traditional teaching methods.

    Statistical Analysis Tool.

    Independent T-Test.

    The study’s data were evaluated using the Independent T-test to compare the mean results of summative tests between two sections, 8-Mercury, and 8-Mars. This test helps identify significant differences in learning outcomes between the experimental group, which tested the game, and the controlled group, which received traditional lessons.

    05. Result and Discussion.

    Paired T-Test for Pre- and Post Test Results.

    The study aimed to determine if using Dr. Abel's Adventure significantly improved the experimental group's learning outcomes. The hypothesis was that the post-test scores would be higher than the pre-test scores. To test this, a paired t-test was conducted on the pre-test and post-test results. The results from the four-day experiment showed that on Day 1, the post-test scores (Mean=9.0278, SD=1.1081) were significantly higher than the pre-test scores (Mean=7.5833, SD=1.6966). Day 2 showed no significant difference, with post-test scores (Mean=8.3404, SD=1.7229) slightly higher than pre-test scores (Mean=7.9574, SD=1.4136). Day 3 and Day 4 results both showed significant improvements, with post-test scores greater than pre-test scores on both days (Day 3: Post-test Mean=7.9545, SD=1.5243 vs. Pre-test Mean=7.2727, SD=1.7962; Day 4: Post-test Mean=7.3261, SD=2.0446 vs. Pre-test Mean=6.4130, SD=1.6677). Overall, the game-based system improved learning outcomes on three out of four days (75%), indicating a satisfactory or average performance.

    Independent T-Test for Summative Test Results.

    The study also aimed to compare the learning outcomes between the experimental group (8-Mars), who used the game, and the control group (8-Mercury), who followed the traditional teaching method. The hypothesis was that the experimental group would score higher on the summative test. To test this, an independent t-test was conducted on the summative test scores of both groups. The results showed that while the experimental group (8-Mars) had a higher mean score (Mean=20.3333, SD=2.7031) compared to the control group (8-Mercury) with a mean score of 19.8000 (SD=1.9141), there was no sufficient evidence to conclude that the difference in scores was statistically significant. Therefore, the game did not show a significant improvement over the traditional teaching method based on the summative test results.

    06. Conclusion.

    With the poor health literacy that the Philippines is facing despite the current efforts of the government to integrate health-related subjects in the DepEd curriculum to teach the students as early as possible, finding other means to educate the people can be helpful to increase the reach of their efforts in improving the country’s health literacy. This study aimed to use the popularity of video games to the masses to create an accessible game-based learning tool and determine its effectiveness in teaching topics about the Prevention and Control of Communicable Diseases. By creating Dr. Abel’s Adventure and testing it on the students of Camarines Sur National High School, the study has found that using Game-based learning in teaching topics about Prevention and Control of Communicable diseases helped improve the learners’ knowledge and awareness about the subject. Although the learning outcome of the Experimental group that tested the game does not significantly differ from the learning outcome of the Control group, the study was still able to serve its original purpose, which was to help enhance its players’ health literacy. Additionally, the findings from this study have shown the importance of having thorough instructions and introduction of the game to the users in providing a good user experience. Implementing a rundown on each element in the game can help the users accomplish actions in the game easily, even on their first try.
