    01. Introduction.

    Project Overview.

    Shift Travel is a user-centric UI/UX project simplifying travel with clear, step-by-step guidance. It consolidates routes, transport options, and real-time updates into an intuitive interface for seamless journeys.

    Responsibilities.

    • UI/UX Designer
    • Technopreneurship

    Project Duration.

    July 2022

    Tools.

    • Figma
    • Lucid Chart

    02. Problem Statement.

    Identifying the Problem.

    Traveling, especially for solo travelers and backpackers, can be stressful due to unclear, fragmented guidance. Existing platforms like Google Maps lack critical details such as local fees, transportation options, and alternative routes. A solution is needed that offers clear, step-by-step navigation along with essential travel information like costs and real-time updates in one cohesive platform.

    03. Goals

    The project aims to create an intuitive UI for solo travelers and backpackers, providing detailed, reliable directions to popular tourist destinations in the Philippines. It includes transportation options, travel times, entry fees, and other crucial details often missing in traditional navigation tools. Additionally, it seeks to align with Technopreneurship principles by offering an innovative travel solution with strong business potential.

    05. Learning and Takeaway.

    One of the key takeaways from this project is the understanding that as a designer or developer, I am not creating for myself, but for others. My professor often emphasizes, "Once you enter the IT world, you're not designing or creating for yourself, but for your users." This is an essential mindset to adopt, especially when designing user interfaces. It's crucial to consider the needs, preferences, and challenges of the target audience to ensure the solution is truly effective and usable.


    For this project, I will be designing a tool for solo and backpack travelersâ€”people with unique needs and experiences. Therefore, I must prioritize usability and accessibility in the design, ensuring that the interface is simple to navigate, visually appealing, and intuitive, even for those unfamiliar with technology.


    Additionally, another important learning is the necessity of designing for the CLI (Common Look and Feel) in the tech industry. This means ensuring that the design adheres to standard user interface principles, creating a consistent, familiar experience that users can quickly adapt to. It's not just about aesthetics but also about providing a seamless experience across devices and platforms, especially in the context of travel, where users might be using the app in unfamiliar environments with varying internet connections.
